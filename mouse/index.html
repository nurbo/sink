Our housing estate

Despite our new and expensively renovated sewage system, the estate smelled. Hidden in the valley, shielded from winter winds and radiation, our community was sheltered from all manner of natural disasters and their ensuing patent thieves. However, the annual heatwave invariably brought a lack of air circulation, stagnant water, slow wifi and abstract conversations about doing something about it.

Already for some time the town had been falling into summer apathy, which we tried to remedy with numerous tasks of little or no relation to each other. With only the rasps of a breathless wind, the lifeless air lounged over the streets. The slopes of the surrounding mountains seemed particularly gentle, like the curves of resting giants and even the clouds rolled slowly — lackadaisical and defensive: their puffy reflections floating in the city pond like boiled cauliflowers. The worst, however, was the fetor of dead rodents, which sought out our traps and filled the air with a sweet, sickly hum.

We had always been told how lucky we were to be born in between the Plagues, in times of political stability, financial liberalism and forward-thinking technologies. Yet any kind of prosperity inevitably comes to an end, which, unlikely as it might seem, we awaited with growing impatience and poorly concealed excitement. Some of us felt that we were born either too late or too early to throw ourselves into history and fully realise our potential.

On good days, I was a predator… motionless… in anticipation of prey to come — seeking reward for my waiting and giving sense to all that sitting and staring. Other days, I would frantically seek out my hidden potential in the patterns of everyday choices. I would imagine all the great or scary deeds I could perform, controversial decisions I would be forced to make and secrets I would not reveal, even if tortured.

Infestation

The Grand Final

To my surprise, the great hall was deserted, those few who arrived, clearly confused, avoided each other's sight. Rows of screen savers were flickering stupefyingly.
